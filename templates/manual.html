🚀 Quick Start Guide For Beginners

New to Stock Hunter? Follow these steps to find your first stock pick in under 5 minutes.

1

Run a Scan

Go to the Dashboard and click "Run Scan". This fetches data for all 859 Shariah-compliant stocks and takes about 1–2 minutes. The results are cached for 24 hours, so you only need to scan once per day.

2

Check the Market Mood

Look at the KLCI Sentiment banner at the top of the Scanner page. If the market is Bullish, conditions favour buying. If Bearish, be extra cautious — consider waiting or focusing on low-risk picks only.

3

Filter for BUY Signals

On the Scanner page, click the "BUY" filter button. This shows only stocks with BUY or STRONG BUY signals. For even safer picks, click "✓ Confirmed" — these are stocks that appeared as BUY in consecutive scans.

4

Look at Grade + Risk

Focus on stocks with Grade A or Grade B — these have the strongest combination of score, volume, trend alignment, and low risk. Avoid stocks with Grade F or High Risk badges. Hover over the grade letter to see exactly why it was assigned.

5

Open Stock Detail

Click any stock row to see the Stock Detail page. Here you'll find the chart, support/resistance levels, entry plan with stop-loss and take-profit prices, and suggested position size. Read the Signal Reasons to understand why the stock was flagged.

6

Set Your Stop-Loss

This is the most important step. On the stock detail page, note the Entry Plan section. It shows a stop-loss price (e.g. RM 2.25) and take-profit target (e.g. RM 2.87). Always set your stop-loss before buying. If you don't know where to exit, don't enter.

7

Track Your Picks

After buying, add the stock to your Portfolio with buy price and quantity. Stock Hunter will track your P&L and alert you if a SELL signal appears. Check the Signal Tracker page regularly to see how past signals performed.

Quick Decision Checklist ✅

✅ Market is Bullish or Neutral
✅ Signal is BUY or STRONG BUY
✅ Grade is A or B
✅ Risk level is Low or Medium
✅ Volume ratio ≥ 1.5x (confirms interest)
✅ Entry Plan R:R ratio ≥ 1.5
✅ Stop-loss is set before buying
✅ Position size ≤ 15% of capital

💡 Aim for at least 6 out of 8 checks before entering a trade.

2. Dashboard

The dashboard is your home screen. It shows:

  • KLCI Sentiment — Current KLCI index price, daily change, market mood (Bullish/Bearish/Neutral), and trend direction. Helps you gauge overall market conditions before buying.
  • Summary Cards — Total stocks scanned, number of BUY/WATCH/SELL signals at a glance.

💡 Tip: Click "Run Scan" to fetch fresh data. Scan results are cached for 24 hours and persist across app restarts.

Click any column header (Score, Price, RSI, ADX, Vol, Div%, Risk, Grade) to sort ascending/descending. The ▲▼ arrow shows current sort direction.

Pagination

Results show 50 per page by default. Use Prev/Next buttons or change to 100, 200, or Show All from the dropdown.

4. Stock Detail

Click any stock row in the scanner to open its detail page. Here you'll find:

  • Interactive Chart — Candlestick chart with EMA 20/50/200, Bollinger Bands, volume bars, MACD, and RSI sub-charts.
  • Technical Indicators — Full breakdown of all computed indicators with current values.
  • Signal Analysis — Detailed BUY/SELL reasons explaining why the score was assigned.
  • Support & Resistance — Key price levels identified from recent price action.
  • Entry Plan — ATR-based stop-loss (price − 2×ATR), take-profit (price + 3×ATR), and risk/reward ratio.
  • Position Sizing — Suggested lot size based on RM10,000 capital and Bursa's 100-share lot.
  • Risk Score — Detailed risk assessment with specific warnings.

Shows sector rotation analysis across 11 Bursa Malaysia sectors (Technology, Consumer, Industrial, Property, Finance, etc.). Each sector displays average performance, number of BUY signals, and trending direction. Use this to identify which sectors are leading/lagging the market.

Lists stocks with unusual volume activity (significantly above their 20-day average). Volume spikes often precede major price moves — they can signal institutional buying/selling, news catalysts, or breakouts. Sorted by volume ratio (highest spike first).

💡 Tip: A volume spike combined with a BUY signal is a strong confirmation. Check the stock detail page for full analysis.

9. Signal Tracker

Tracks how well BUY signals performed over time. After a BUY signal is generated, the tracker checks the actual stock price at 7, 14, and 30 days later.

  • Win Rate — Percentage of signals that were profitable at each time period
  • Average Return — Mean return across all tracked signals
  • Best/Worst — Top and bottom performing signals

Win Rate by Score Range

Shows historical win rates grouped by the stock's score at the time of the BUY signal (based on 14-day outcomes). This helps you decide which signals are worth acting on:

Score 60+ Strong Buy Zone — highest confidence signals. Historically the best performers.
Score 35–59 Buy Zone — good signals with moderate confidence. Use additional confirmation (Grade, volume).
Score 10–34 Watch Zone — weaker signals. Better suited for watchlist than immediate action.
💡 Tip: If the 7-day win rate is consistently above 50%, the scanner's signals are reliable. Use the "Win Rate by Score Range" card to identify which score levels have the best track record — then only act on signals in those ranges.

Signal Thresholds

STRONG BUY: score ≥ 50 BUY: score ≥ 25 WATCH: score ≥ 10 HOLD: score > -10 SELL: score ≤ -10 STRONG SELL: score ≤ -30

12. Confidence Grades

The Confidence Grade (A–F) is a single at-a-glance rating that combines five factors into one letter. It helps you quickly assess whether a signal is worth acting on without interpreting individual indicators.

How Grades Are Calculated

Each factor contributes points on a 100-point scale:

Factor Max Points What It Checks
Score Strength 40 How strong is the net score? (70+ = 40pts, 55+ = 30pts, 35+ = 20pts)
Volume Confirmation 20 Is volume backing the move? (2x+ = 20pts, 1.5x+ = 15pts)
Multi-Timeframe 20 Do daily & weekly charts agree? (Both bullish = 20pts)
Risk Level 10 How risky is the entry? (Low = 10pts, Medium = 5pts)
Confirmation 10 Did the BUY signal appear in consecutive scans? (+10pts)

Grade Meanings

A High Confidence (75+ pts) — Strong setup, multiple confirmations
B Good (55–74 pts) — Solid setup, most factors align
C Moderate (35–54 pts) — Mixed signals, proceed with caution
D Weak (20–34 pts) — Few confirmations, high uncertainty
F Avoid (<20 pts) — Weak or conflicting signals
💡 Tip: For beginners, stick to Grade A and B stocks only. These have the best alignment of score, volume, trend, and risk — giving you the highest probability of a successful trade.

13. Risk Management

Stock Hunter includes several tools to help minimize risk:

Risk Score

Each stock is rated Low / Medium / High based on:

  • ATR volatility > 5% of price (+3 points)
  • RSI > 75 overbought (+3 points)
  • ADX < 15 no clear trend (+2 points)
  • Volume < 0.5× average low liquidity (+2 points)
  • Near 52-week high (+3 points)

Score ≥5 = High risk, ≥2 = Medium, <2 = Low

Entry Plan (ATR-based)

  • Stop-Loss = Current price − (2 × ATR) — limits your downside
  • Take-Profit = Current price + (3 × ATR) — targets 1.5:1 reward-to-risk
  • Risk/Reward Ratio — Green if ≥1.5 (good), red if <1 (avoid)

⚠️ Risk Warnings on Scanner

Yellow ⚠️ icons appear next to each stock with specific warnings: overbought RSI, low volume, near 52-week high, high volatility, or no clear trend.
