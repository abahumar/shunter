🚀 Quick Start Guide For Beginners

New to Stock Hunter? Follow these steps to find your first stock pick in under 5 minutes.

1

Run a Scan

Go to the Dashboard and click "Run Scan". This fetches data for all 859 Shariah-compliant stocks and takes about 1–2 minutes. The results are cached for 24 hours, so you only need to scan once per day.

2

Check the Market Mood

Look at the KLCI Sentiment banner at the top of the Scanner page. If the market is Bullish, conditions favour buying. If Bearish, be extra cautious — consider waiting or focusing on low-risk picks only.

3

Filter for BUY Signals

On the Scanner page, click the "BUY" filter button. This shows only stocks with BUY or STRONG BUY signals. For even safer picks, click "✓ Confirmed" — these are stocks that appeared as BUY in consecutive scans.

4

Look at Grade + Risk

Focus on stocks with Grade A or Grade B — these have the strongest combination of score, volume, trend alignment, and low risk. Avoid stocks with Grade F or High Risk badges. Hover over the grade letter to see exactly why it was assigned.

5

Open Stock Detail

Click any stock row to see the Stock Detail page. Here you'll find the chart, support/resistance levels, entry plan with stop-loss and take-profit prices, and suggested position size. Read the Signal Reasons to understand why the stock was flagged.

6

Set Your Stop-Loss

This is the most important step. On the stock detail page, note the Entry Plan section. It shows a stop-loss price (e.g. RM 2.25) and take-profit target (e.g. RM 2.87). Always set your stop-loss before buying. If you don't know where to exit, don't enter.

7

Track Your Picks

After buying, add the stock to your Portfolio with buy price and quantity. Stock Hunter will track your P&L and alert you if a SELL signal appears. Check the Signal Tracker page regularly to see how past signals performed.

Quick Decision Checklist ✅

✅ Market is Bullish or Neutral
✅ Signal is BUY or STRONG BUY
✅ Grade is A or B
✅ Risk level is Low or Medium
✅ Volume ratio ≥ 1.5x (confirms interest)
✅ Entry Plan R:R ratio ≥ 1.5
✅ Stop-loss is set before buying
✅ Position size ≤ 15% of capital

💡 Aim for at least 6 out of 8 checks before entering a trade.

Setup Holding Description

12. Confidence Grades

The Confidence Grade (A–F) is a single at-a-glance rating that combines five factors into one letter. It helps you quickly assess whether a signal is worth acting on without interpreting individual indicators.

How Grades Are Calculated

Each factor contributes points on a 100-point scale:

Factor Max Points What It Checks
Score Strength 40 How strong is the net score? (70+ = 40pts, 55+ = 30pts, 35+ = 20pts)
Volume Confirmation 20 Is volume backing the move? (2x+ = 20pts, 1.5x+ = 15pts)
Multi-Timeframe 20 Do daily & weekly charts agree? (Both bullish = 20pts)
Risk Level 10 How risky is the entry? (Low = 10pts, Medium = 5pts)
Confirmation 10 Did the BUY signal appear in consecutive scans? (+10pts)

Grade Meanings

A High Confidence (75+ pts) — Strong setup, multiple confirmations
B Good (55–74 pts) — Solid setup, most factors align
C Moderate (35–54 pts) — Mixed signals, proceed with caution
D Weak (20–34 pts) — Few confirmations, high uncertainty
F Avoid (<20 pts) — Weak or conflicting signals
💡 Tip: For beginners, stick to Grade A and B stocks only. These have the best alignment of score, volume, trend, and risk — giving you the highest probability of a successful trade.

14. Volume Price Analysis (VPA) 📊

What is VPA?

Imagine you're at a pasar malam (night market). If a stall has a long queue (high volume) and people are happily paying full price (close near top), that stall has real demand — the food is genuinely good. But if there's a long queue and people are walking away without buying (close near bottom), something is wrong — maybe the seller is dumping old stock.

VPA reads the stock market the same way. It looks at three things on every candle:

  1. Volume — How many people showed up? (high, low, or ultra-high)
  2. Spread — How far did price move? (wide candle = big move, narrow = small)
  3. Close position — Where did price end up? (near the top = buyers won, near bottom = sellers won)

By combining these three clues, VPA reveals what smart money (big institutional players) is doing — are they quietly accumulating (buying) or distributing (selling) before the crowd notices?

VPA Patterns Explained

Pattern Signal What's Happening Real-World Analogy
Accumulation 🟢 Bullish High volume + price closes near the top of the candle Big buyers quietly filling their bags — they believe the price will go up
Distribution 🔴 Bearish High volume + price closes near the bottom of the candle Big players selling to eager buyers — "buy my stock, it's great!" (while they exit)
Selling Climax 🟢 Bullish Ultra-high volume + wide down bar but closes in upper half Panic selling exhausted — like a fire sale where smart money swoops in and buys everything cheap
Buying Climax 🔴 Bearish Ultra-high volume + wide up bar but closes in lower half FOMO frenzy — everyone rushed to buy, but smart money used the excitement to sell to them
No Supply 🟢 Bullish Low volume + small down bar (narrow spread) Price dipped a little but nobody is selling — sellers are exhausted, ready to go up
No Demand 🔴 Bearish Low volume + small up bar (narrow spread) Price went up a tiny bit but nobody cares — no real buyers, likely to fall
Test 🟢 Bullish Low-volume dip into support followed by a recovery bar Market "tests" if sellers are still around — low volume means they're gone, safe to go up
Upthrust 🔴 Bearish Push above previous highs on high volume but closes weak (near bottom) Bull trap — price broke out but fell back. Smart money used the breakout to sell
Effort No Result ⚠️ Warning High volume but narrow spread (price barely moved) Lots of activity but price didn't move — buying and selling are in a tug-of-war. Direction depends on the bar: up bar = bearish (supply absorbing), down bar = bullish (demand absorbing)

How VPA Works in Stock Hunter

The scanner analyzes the last 5 candles of every stock and looks for VPA patterns. Here's what you see:

  • VPA column — Shows the most significant pattern detected (e.g., "Accumulation" in green, "Distribution" in red)
  • VPA Score — Hover over the pattern to see the score. Positive = bullish pressure, negative = bearish pressure
  • Net Score boost — VPA contributes up to ±15 points to the stock's net score, directly improving or reducing its signal grade
  • 📊 VPA Bullish filter — Click to show only stocks with bullish VPA patterns (Accumulation, No Supply, Test, Selling Climax)

Step-by-Step: Using VPA as a Beginner

  1. Run a scan, then click 📊 VPA Bullish to filter stocks with bullish patterns
  2. Look at the VPA column — "Accumulation" is the best bullish signal. It means big players are buying
  3. Cross-check with Grade and Signal — A stock showing Accumulation + Grade A/B + BUY signal is a high-conviction pick.
    Think of it like this: the scanner says BUY, the grade confirms quality, and VPA confirms smart money agrees
  4. Watch out for Distribution on stocks you own — If a stock in your portfolio shows "Distribution", smart money may be exiting. Consider tightening your stop-loss
  5. Use VPA in backtest — Go to Backtest page and check 📊 VPA Confirm to see how requiring bullish VPA patterns affects your win rate
✅ Good Signs (Bullish VPA)
  • • Accumulation → Smart money buying
  • • No Supply → Sellers exhausted
  • • Test → Support confirmed
  • • Selling Climax → Panic over, reversal likely
⛔ Warning Signs (Bearish VPA)
  • • Distribution → Smart money selling
  • • No Demand → Nobody wants to buy
  • • Upthrust → Fake breakout, bull trap
  • • Buying Climax → FOMO peak, about to reverse
💡 Tip: VPA is most powerful when combined with other indicators. A stock with Accumulation + Grade A + STRONG BUY + Confirmed is about as confident as it gets. Don't trade on VPA alone — always check the full picture. If VPA says bullish but everything else says sell, trust the majority.

15. Risk Management

Stock Hunter includes several tools to help minimize risk:

Risk Score

Each stock is rated Low / Medium / High based on:

  • ATR volatility > 5% of price (+3 points)
  • RSI > 75 overbought (+3 points)
  • ADX < 15 no clear trend (+2 points)
  • Volume < 0.5× average low liquidity (+2 points)
  • Near 52-week high (+3 points)

Score ≥5 = High risk, ≥2 = Medium, <2 = Low

Entry Plan (ATR-based)

  • Stop-Loss = Current price − (2 × ATR) — limits your downside
  • Take-Profit = Current price + (3 × ATR) — targets 1.5:1 reward-to-risk
  • Risk/Reward Ratio — Green if ≥1.5 (good), red if <1 (avoid)

⚠️ Risk Warnings on Scanner

Yellow ⚠️ icons appear next to each stock with specific warnings: overbought RSI, low volume, near 52-week high, high volatility, or no clear trend.
